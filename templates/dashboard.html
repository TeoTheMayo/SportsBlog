How the Rams Bought a Super Bowl, and reinvested their winnings into a rebuild.

Sam Liftin

The NFL is changing and more than ever as we see the need for team’s to develop their homegrown talent and ensure they make the right picks in the draft. While this has always been the case, with defensive schemes improving each year, having an elite quarterback does not guarantee success as it once did. In today’s NFL having elite players on rookie deals is the only real way to constantly compete for championships. This is most evident when looking at the Philadelphia Eagles who won Super Bowl 59 due to having talent on rookie deals such as Jalen Carter, Nokibe Dean, Cooper Dejean, and Quinton Mitchell. The Eagles do pay their star players but Howie Roseman has gained a reputation as one of the best General Managers in the NFL because of the roster he built. The Eagles also have retained many players whom they drafted, establishing most of the roster from the draft. However, this process is too long for some teams so they attempt to buy a championship and the most recent case of this would be the Long Angeles Rams. The Rams victory in Super Bowl 56 against the Cincinnati Bengals was notably notably traded for Matthew Stafford, Odel Beckham Jr, and Von Miller; all crucial factors in the team’s super bowl journey. While the super team strategy worked for the Rams in 2022, the following season they struggled greatly, and their lack of first round draft picks in the previous season hurt them greatly. They had an awful year in 2023, due to injuries, and lack of contribution from young players. However, after that horrendous season the Rams managed to make the playoffs back to back seasons the following year. While Coach Sean MccVay, and Quarterback Matthew Stafford, deserve the majority of the credit for the past two seasons, a large amount of credit is due to the young players that stepped up for the Rams. Most notably Puka Nuka, and Kyren Williams who have helped completely revamp the Rams offense. Keep in mind these guys were not taken high on the draft board. Both players were taken in the 5th round, Kyren in the 2022 draft, and Puka in the 2023 draft. On the defensive side, picks such as DT Kobie Turner, and DROTY Jarved Verse transformed the defense. The Rams currently have the youngest, and least expensive defense so the future is very bright.
